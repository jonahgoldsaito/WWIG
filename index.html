What Would I Get (If I Followed You)

WWIG takes a Twitter user's timeline and fades out the ones that wouldn't cross your stream, giving you more of a sense of what you'd see daily if you clicked "follow."

It also gives you some stats (e.g. 3.9 Tweets / Day) so you know a little bit more about the user you're looking at.

Grab the following bookmarklet, try it on any user's timeline on twitter.com.

WWIG

What do you get with WWIG?

  1. All the @ tweets faded so you can see the real meat and potatoes. Notice that most of @paul_irish's visible tweets probably wouldn't come up in your feed.
  2. Relevant Stats: the user's tweets per day, and the tweets per day that you'd actually see
  3. A preview of what said-user would look like with a wig. muy importante.

Most of the stuff I want to see when I go to a Twitter timeline is drowned in a crap heap of things I don't want to see. A lot of those tweets will never grace my timeline.

I just want to quickly glance and see what I'd get (WWIG) if I followed you. The bookmarklet also adds some stats - a rough estimate of how many of your tweets I'd get per day. That seems pretty relevant.

Simple. Just something I wished Twitter did natively.
